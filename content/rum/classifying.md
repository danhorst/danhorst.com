In [_Smuggler's Cove_][1], Martin Cate, proprietor of the [renowned tiki bar in San Francisco of the same name][2], groups the [dimensions of rum][3] into the following categories:

- Pot still unaged
- ① _Pot still lightly aged_
- Pot still aged
- Pot still long aged
- ② _Blended lightly aged_
- ③ _Blended aged_
- Blended long aged
- Column still lightly aged
- ④ _Column still aged_
- Column still long aged
- Black pot still
- ⑤ _Black blended_
- ⑥ _Black blended overproof_
- Cane Coffey still aged
- Cane Pot still unaged
- ⑦ _Cane AOC Martinique rhum agricole blanc_
- ⑧ _Cane AOC Martinique rhum agricole vieux_
- Cane AOC Martinique rhum agricole long aged
- Pot still unaged cachaça
- Pot still aged cachaça

All varieties of rum have their uses but the 8 italicized classifications form the backbone of rum-based cocktails. Unaged rums are excluded from the core cocktail varieties as they are harsh spirits and thus difficult to blend. The nuances of long-aged rums only shine in very simple cocktails or, to be proper, they should be sipped neat.
