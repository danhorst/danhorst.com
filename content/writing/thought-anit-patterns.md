I struggle with imposter syndrome[^1].
In my head I maintain the illusion that I don't have value, can't accomplish anything, or would never be welcomed into `DESIRABLE_ORGANIZATION`[^2] using a combination of mental gymnastics and the fallacies of my own brain.
I'm listing them here to help me, and hopefully you, avoid them in the future[^3].

## Expertise Blindness

When you know something really well, inside and out[^4], both simple facts and complex ideas in that domain are obvious to you.
It's easy to assume that things you find obvious are also perceived as obvious to the people around you.
That incorrect assumption devalues the knowledge you've gained through curiosity, perseverance, and lived experience.

## Galaxy Brain Depression

It is impossible to know _everything_ even if you limit yourself to a tiny subject area. Even if it _were_ possible to know _everything_ at a given moment things grow, expand, and sometimes radically change over time. You will be wrong about almost everything in some way or on some timescale.

## It's Forests All the Way Down

The world is spectacularly interconnected. There are lifetimes of possibility in every detail. You will be a beginner and make beginner mistakes 

## If Can't Do It Right Don't Do It At All

Perfect is the enemy of done.
