In my day job, I am a Divisional Architect for central IT at a well-known university. A Divisional Architect is`an Enterprise Architect that focuses on the needs of a group of the departments. To do this well I _also_ have to know as much as possible about the needs and direction of the organization as a whole. With that information in hand, I can ensure that our divisional initiatives properly align with the rest of the organization.

## The Universal Mandate of IT Organizations
IT organizations have a dual mandate:

1. To decrease the marginal cost of service delivery over time
2. To improve the business functions of the organization through improvements in the service portfolio

Let's break this down a bit. First, a "service" is any process or software that facilitaes or accomplishes an organizational business need. Any software used by an organization can be classified as a service be it vended solutions, custom applications, databases, or (god forbid) spreadsheets full of pivot tables. How these services are managed and operated falls under the purview of these two acronyms:

- ITSM: _Information Technology Service Management_
- ITSD: _Information Technology Service Delivery_

It many organizations, these two concerns are addressed using the ITIL model (ITIL stands for _Information Technology Infrastructure Library_ but it really just a name for a specific operational and organizational framework).

## The Role of Enterprise Architecture
When I was in my 20s I looked at enterprise architects with borderline contempt. The examples I saw were all old white men that created, and argued over, architecture diagrams with little-to-no indication that they had mastered the tools and techniques required to actually accomplish the work they had outlined.

### Looking Forward, Looking Inward

The vanguard and the foundation


A Software Architect helps define how a particular piece of software is constructed. This involves the language, tooling, frameworks, design patterns, and external libraries used to craft the final solution. To my knowledge, we don't have any positions in the OIT that are solely dedicated to a Software Architect role.

Instead, we have architectural design roles based on which administrative unit is the primary concern:
Enterprise Architects serve the OIT as a whole with an eye towards campus
Divisional Enterprise Architects (sometimes referred to as Domain Architects) serve a division of the OIT with an eye towards the OIT as a whole (and half an eye towards campus).
In both cases, these roles have the same fundamental concerns:
The vanguard: look at industry trends and exemplary peers to help the organization prepare for the future.
The foundation: continually assess the operational reality and help the organization realize its potential through changes in people, process, and tools. 
In that light, Architectural Design is the practice of guiding the lifecycle of individual services so that the overall service portfolio will meet current and future needs.
